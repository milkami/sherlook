The chassis features a novel hybrid design, combining a composite monocoque with a steel frame. A lightweight, strong carbon fiber reinforced plastic (CFRP) monocoque is constructed at the front, housing the driver's survival cell. This new, in-house developed monocoque has a lower center of gravity, adjustable pedals, and custom-molded seat padding for comfort. A compartment beneath the driver's seat holds the ECU, fuel tank, accumulator, and electronic sensors, accessible by detaching an inner firewall. The monocoque extends to the front suspension mounting points for improved rigidity. Designed with hybrid systems in mind, dedicated space is provided for the accumulator and motor controller units. The monocoque is built using prepreg, autoclave technology, and aluminum honeycomb for stiffness, featuring UD and twill layers, and three optimized insert types at load points. The rear of the car has a steel tubular space frame for easy powertrain maintenance, supporting the engine, drivetrain, and suspension units. The design allows detachment with six bolts, and quick connectors for wiring and hydraulic hoses. FEA simulations and topological optimization ensure strength and reduced weight.
